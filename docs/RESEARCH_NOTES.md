# Research Notes

This project is based on common influencer marketing workflow needs:

- Teams need to move from manual creator discovery to repeatable shortlisting.
- Follower count alone is a weak proxy for creator quality.
- View-based engagement, content relevance, commerce readiness, and brand safety should be scored separately.
- Audience demographics are often incomplete, so the system should show confidence levels instead of pretending all signals are precise.
- Human review should remain part of the final decision.

## Platform-Aware Research

Creator fit depends on the platform context of the brand. The same product can require different storytelling patterns across channels:

- Facebook often shows community trust, long-copy narrative, comments, reviews, and local objections.
- YouTube reveals long-form education depth, script structure, chapters, and retention hooks.
- TikTok reveals short-form hooks, scene pacing, creator-native language, caption style, and CTA rhythm.
- Instagram reveals visual identity, lifestyle framing, reels, highlights, and aesthetic consistency.
- X reveals concise positioning, topic velocity, direct feedback, and potential risk signals.
- The website shows owned positioning, conversion structure, claims, pricing, and proof points.

The recommended production flow is to ingest each platform separately, extract scripts and scene-level signals, synthesize the brand context, and only then recommend creator roles.

Potential data-source categories:

- Public creator profile and content data
- Authorized API data
- Internal CRM and campaign history
- First-party performance feedback
- Manual research notes from strategist teams
